## The $200 minimum viable ergonomic upgrade

If you can only spend $200 total, buy in this order and stop when you hit the budget:

| # | Item | Budget | Pick |
|---|------|--------|------|
| 1 | External keyboard | $40–60 | Logitech K380 or Keychron K3 |
| 2 | External mouse | $30–50 | Logitech M720 Triathlon or MX Anywhere 3 |
| 3 | Laptop stand | $35–45 | Nexstand K2 or Amazon Basics adjustable |
| 4 | Task lamp | $40 | TaoTronics DL16 or BlitzWolf BW-LT1 |

**Total: ~$150–195.** See the [decision guide](/research/laptop-stand-vs-monitor-arm) for why this specific order.

## Chairs

### Under $250

| Product | Price | Buy for | Skip if |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| Branch Ergonomic Chair | ~$199–249 | Adjustable lumbar + 3D arms at this price | You need warranty >7yr |
| HON Ignition 2.0 | ~$230–280 | Proven longevity, 10yr warranty | Lumbar depth doesn't fit you (not adjustable) |
| Sihoo M57 / Doro C300 | ~$180–260 | Cheapest with adjustable lumbar + 3D arms | You can't return easily (mechanism failure reports) |

### $250–$400

| Product | Price | Buy for | Skip if |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| Autonomous ErgoChair Pro | ~$349–449 | Full adjustments in one package | You need >2yr warranty |
| FlexiSpot BS series (specific SKUs) | ~$300–450 | Solid value if you pick the right SKU | You won't read individual SKU reviews |
| Steelcase Series 1 (refurbished) | ~$350–450 | Best value in the entire guide | You can't accept cosmetic wear |

### $400–$500

| Product | Price | Buy for | Skip if |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| Steelcase Series 1 (new) | ~$415–500 | 12yr warranty, proven mechanism | You prefer a more forgiving seat foam |
| Haworth Fern (refurbished) | ~$400–500 | Best-in-class flexible back | You can't find one in stock |
| Herman Miller Sayl (base) | ~$495 | Iconic design if you love it | You haven't sat in one first |

### Chairs to avoid at any price under $500

- Any "gaming" chair with racing-bucket bolsters (Secretlab, DXRacer, Corsair, etc.) — geometry defect for 8-hour work
- Any "executive high-back leather" with fixed lumbar — furniture, not a tool
- Kneeling / saddle chairs as *primary* seating
- Balance-ball chairs as primary seating (Cochrane-level evidence against)
- Drop-shipped "ergonomic" chairs from Instagram/TikTok white-label brands

*Full reasoning: [Ergonomic Chair Under $500 Guide](/research/ergonomic-chair-under-500)*

## Monitor Arms

| Product | Price | Buy for | Skip if |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| Amazon Basics Single Monitor Arm | ~$40 | Cheapest workable option | You have a monitor >15lb |
| North Bayou F80 | ~$40–60 | Best sub-$60 option | You care about looks / mount feel |
| VIVO / Huanuo mid-range | ~$60–90 | Better build than Amazon Basics | You want long-term (>5yr) reliability |
| Jarvis Monitor Arm | ~$150–200 | Fully adjustable, honest quality | You need 20lb+ capacity |
| Ergotron LX | ~$180–220 | Industry standard, 10yr warranty | Monitor >20lb |
| Ergotron HX | ~$280–380 | Only sub-$400 arm for heavy 34"+ ultrawides | Standard-size monitors |
| Herman Miller Ollin | ~$375–475 | Best-in-class fluid motion | Budget constraint |

### Monitor arms to avoid at any price

- Ball-joint desk arms (loosen, sag, cannot hold heavier monitors)
- Suction-cup mounts (fail catastrophically over months)
- Sub-$60 gas-spring arms with unknown-brand cylinders
- Wall-mounted arms on sit/stand desks (defeats the point)

## The full "avoid at any price" list, consolidated

If you take nothing else from the site, avoid these:

1. Gaming chairs with racing bolsters for full-day work
2. Ball-joint monitor arms and suction-cup monitor mounts
3. Cooling pads used as laptop stands
4. Ergonomic keyboards without split-key or negative-tilt geometry
5. Gaming mice over 100g for full-day use
6. Blue-light-blocking desk lamps as ergonomic intervention
7. Bare LED strips as primary task lighting
8. Gaming headsets for 8-hour wear
9. IEM headsets for full-day use
10. Balance-ball chairs as primary seating
11. Any product where the vendor won't disclose specifications you can verify
